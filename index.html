About Me

Yamkela Lusithi is a Digital Learning Developer, with a strong focus on designing and developing learning interfaces that improve and support digital literacy, learner engagement, and academic achievement. My focus is on developing inclusive, accessible, and effective learning environments by leveraging LMS platforms, Frontend Development, and research-informed thinking. I'm passionate about blending pedagogy, research, and innovation to ensure technology truly enhances how learners access, process, and apply knowledge.

Educational Philosophy

I believe that technology should not replace teaching, but rather extend its impact. As a Digital Learning Developer, I focus on learner-centered, inclusive, and evidence-based practices that integrate digital tools seamlessly into teaching and learning.

My philosophy is that effective learning must be guided by pedagogy, not novelty. I aim to empower learners by using technology to cultivate digital literacy, critical thinking, and lifelong learning skills while ensuring accessibility for all students.

EdTech Approach, Practice, and Effectiveness

I approach educational technology as a bridge between pedagogy and innovation. My role involves collaborating with educators to integrate tools that enrich learning experiences and provide students with flexible, engaging opportunities.

Role in Learning: I design interactive interfaces and facilitate digital skill development. My approach emphasizes autonomy, collaboration, and personalized pathways for learners.

Methodology: I use blended learning models, flipped classrooms, and gamified interfaces to drive engagement. Tools such as Google Classroom, Microsoft Teams, and interactive coding projects allow me to design scalable and adaptive solutions.

Planning and Preparation: Each solution is aligned with outcomes, including assessment checkpoints and opportunities for reflection. I prepare multimedia resources, digital platforms, and LMS integrations that make content more interactive and accessible.

Use of Technology: From learning management systems to web-based educational interfaces, I design and implement tools that promote collaboration, critical thinking, and active engagement.

Learner Development and Support

My work prioritizes assisting students in developing digital skills and confidence in using technology. I support learners in bridging gaps between traditional literacy and digital literacy.

Mentorship: Guiding students in using online platforms, developing study strategies, and engaging with Educational Technology confidently.

Workshops: Delivering interactive sessions on tools like Google Classroom, Microsoft Teams, and coding for beginners.

Academic Skills: Supporting learners with digital research, online collaboration, and presentation tools.

Motivation: Using gamified platforms, progress trackers, and feedback systems to sustain engagement and promote ownership of learning.

Assessment Practices with Technology

I design technology driven assessments that provide real-time feedback and align with learning objectives.

  • Formative: Interactive quizzes, polls, and discussion boards.
  • Summative: Digital projects, portfolios, and online presentations.
  • Continuous: Analytics from LMS platforms to track student progress.

I ensure feedback is timely, constructive, and accessible via digital channels, helping learners track their growth.

Reflection and Feedback

I use analytics, peer feedback, and learner input to refine my designs. By reflecting on usage data and student engagement, I continuously iterate and improve digital learning tools.

Professional Development in EdTech

I stay current by engaging in online courses, EdTech communities, and conferences. My focus is on mastering instructional design software, coding, and digital learning research.

Recognitions and certifications in Full Stack Development, CompTIA A+, and TEFL reflect my commitment to combining technical expertise with pedagogy.

Research in Educational Technology

My research investigates how technology impacts literacy, digital competence, and learner engagement. Key areas include:

  • Blended learning in language education.
  • Gamification for literacy development.
  • EdTech tools for differentiated instruction.
